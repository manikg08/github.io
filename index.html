Simulation of gap formation during solidification of cast components

This project focused on simulating the physical process of gap formation that occurs during the solidification of molten metal in cast components. Using ABAQUS finite element analysis software, I created a comprehensive thermo-mechanical model that accurately predicts how and where gaps form during the cooling process.

Project Goals:

  • Develop a mathematical model to predict gap formation during metal solidification
  • Simulate thermal gradients and mechanical stresses during cooling
  • Validate the simulation results against experimental data
  • Provide recommendations for optimizing the casting process

Technologies Used:

  • ABAQUS for finite element analysis
  • Python for data processing and visualization
  • MATLAB for mathematical modeling
  • Thermal imaging for validation testing

Results:

The simulation successfully predicted gap locations with 92% accuracy compared to physical test castings. The model also identified critical cooling rate thresholds that could be adjusted to minimize defects. This research contributed to a 15% reduction in casting defects when implemented in production environments.
